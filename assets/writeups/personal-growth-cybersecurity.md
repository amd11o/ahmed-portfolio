# Personal Growth in Cybersecurity

When I started taking cybersecurity seriously, I thought progress meant collecting tools and certificates only. Later, I learned that the strongest profile is built from four things together:

```text
Learning → Practice → Documentation → Communication
```

A certificate is stronger when it is connected to notes, projects, reports, and real problem-solving.

---

## My current direction

My profile sits between defensive security and offensive security.

On the defensive side, SOC work taught me how to review alerts, read logs, understand context, document evidence, and think about incident impact.

On the offensive side, eJPT-style labs taught me how to move with a methodology: reconnaissance, enumeration, exploitation, privilege escalation, pivoting, and reporting.

That balance helps me understand both sides: how attackers move and how defenders can detect and respond.

---

## What my credentials represent

I do not see certificates as decoration. I see each one as a learning milestone.

- **eJPT** strengthened my practical penetration-testing mindset.
- **CompTIA Security+** strengthened my security foundation and exam discipline.
- **HCIA-Security** helped me organize network security concepts.
- **CCNAv7** improved my networking fundamentals.
- **Microsoft PL-900 and AI-900** added platform, cloud, and AI awareness.
- **Dean’s Awards** show consistency and academic discipline.
- **CTF and hackathon participation** show practical curiosity and willingness to compete.

The goal is not to look busy. The goal is to show growth with evidence.

---

## Why I write notes

I write notes because memory is not reliable during pressure.

Good notes help me:

- repeat a workflow faster
- avoid the same mistake twice
- explain my work clearly
- convert labs into portfolio evidence
- improve my technical English

A clean write-up proves that I understood the work, not only that I finished it.

## Advice I would give myself

Do not wait until you feel perfect.

Build small projects, write small notes, publish safe work, and improve every month.

Cybersecurity rewards consistency more than hype.
