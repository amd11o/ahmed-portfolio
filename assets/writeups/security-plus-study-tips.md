# Security+ Study Tips

Security+ is not only a definition exam. The questions often test whether I can choose the best control for a situation.

My approach is simple: understand the scenario, identify the risk, then choose the control that reduces the risk with the least side effect.

---

## 1. I connect every term to a real use case

Examples:

```text
MFA → protects accounts if a password leaks
Least privilege → limits damage after compromise
Segmentation → slows lateral movement
SIEM → correlates logs for investigation
EDR → detects suspicious endpoint behavior
Backups → support recovery after ransomware
Hashing → integrity check, not confidentiality
Encryption → confidentiality, reversible with a key
Encoding → formatting, not security
```

When I understand the use case, I do not panic when the question wording changes.

---

## 2. Topics I revise the most

- Threats, attacks, and vulnerabilities
- Security architecture and design
- Identity and access management
- Risk management and governance
- Incident response
- Cryptography basics
- Cloud and virtualization security
- Network security controls
- Logging and monitoring
- Business continuity and disaster recovery

I do not leave governance topics until the last day. They appear in many scenario questions.

---

## 3. How I answer questions

My exam habit:

1. Read the last sentence first.
2. Identify what the question asks: prevent, detect, respond, recover, or choose the best option.
3. Remove answers that are true but not relevant.
4. Watch keywords: `best`, `first`, `next`, `most likely`, `least`, `primary`.
5. If two answers look correct, choose the one that solves the root problem.

Security+ likes distractors. I do not choose an answer only because I recognize the word.

---

## 4. Concepts I keep repeating

```text
Authentication = proving identity
Authorization = what the identity can access
Accounting = logs and activity records
Risk = likelihood × impact
Threat = potential cause of harm
Vulnerability = weakness
Exploit = method used against a weakness
Control = protection or mitigation
```

---

## 5. Practice strategy

I prefer small daily practice over one long session.

```text
30–50 questions per day
Review every wrong answer
Write why the correct answer is correct
Write why my selected answer was wrong
Re-test weak domains
```

If I keep missing one topic, I stop doing random questions and study that topic directly.

---

## 6. PBQ mindset

For performance-based questions, I do not rush.

I look for:

- network zones
- firewall direction
- least privilege
- secure protocols
- logs and alerts
- business requirement
- risk priority

I solve the easy parts first, then return to complex parts.

---

## 7. Final advice

Security+ rewards calm thinking. If I understand the control and the scenario, the answer becomes easier.

My goal is not to memorize every sentence. My goal is to understand why each control exists.
